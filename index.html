How worthwhile it is to get yourself up on the track

April 11, 2023 by Jon

Learning to ski is an exciting and rewarding experience. However, it can also be challenging and intimidating, especially for beginners. In this blog, we will provide some tips on how to get up on skis and handle them like an Olympic champion.

Take a Lesson

One of the best ways to learn how to ski is by taking a lesson from a qualified instructor. Ski instructors are trained professionals who can teach you the correct techniques and provide feedback on your form. They can also help you progress at your own pace and ensure that you are skiing safely.

Get the Right Gear

Having the right gear is essential for skiing. Make sure you have the appropriate clothing, including waterproof ski pants and jacket, gloves, and goggles. It's also important to have well-fitted ski boots and skis that are appropriate for your skill level.

Start on the Bunny Slope

When you first start skiing, it's important to begin on a beginner slope or a "bunny slope." These slopes have a gentler incline and are designed for beginners to practice their skills. Take your time and focus on getting comfortable with the sensation of sliding on snow and getting up from a fall.

Use Proper Body Position

Proper body position is crucial for skiing. Your weight should be evenly distributed on both skis, and your knees should be slightly bent. Keep your arms forward and your hands in front of you to help maintain balance.

Practice Makes Perfect

As with any sport, practice is essential for improving your skiing skills. Take advantage of any opportunities to ski, whether it's on a weekend ski trip or during a local ski club's weekly sessions. As you become more comfortable on skis, challenge yourself by tackling steeper slopes or trying out new techniques.

Don't Be Afraid to Fall

Falling is a natural part of learning to ski. Don't be afraid to fall or make mistakes - it's all part of the learning process. Make sure to fall safely by tucking in your limbs and keeping your head up.

In conclusion, learning to ski takes time and practice, but with the right attitude and techniques, anyone can become a skilled skier. Remember to take a lesson from a qualified instructor, use proper body position, start on a beginner slope, and don't be afraid to fall. With these tips, you'll be skiing like an Olympic champion in no time!

How to avoid avalanches and what a skier should do if he or she is hit by an avalanche

Skiing in the mountains can be a thrilling experience, but it also comes with some risks, including the potential for avalanches. In this blog, we will discuss how to avoid avalanches and what to do if you're hit by one while skiing.

How to Avoid Avalanches

Check Avalanche Forecasts

Before heading out for a day of skiing, check the avalanche forecast for the area. Avalanche forecasts provide information on the likelihood of an avalanche occurring, as well as the potential size and danger level.

Stay on Designated Ski Runs

Ski runs that are marked and maintained by the ski resort are typically safe from avalanches. Stay on designated ski runs and avoid skiing in areas marked as off-limits or closed due to avalanche danger.

Ski with a Partner

Skiing with a partner is always a good idea, but it's especially important when skiing in areas with avalanche risk. Make sure to stay within sight of your partner and have a plan in place in case of an emergency.

Carry Avalanche Safety Gear

Carrying avalanche safety gear, such as an avalanche transceiver, shovel, and probe, can be lifesaving in case of an avalanche. Make sure to practice using this equipment before heading out on the slopes.

What to Do if You're Hit by an Avalanche

If you are caught in an avalanche while skiing, here are some steps to take:

  1. Try to Ski out of the Avalanche

    2. If you're caught in a small avalanche, try to ski out of it by staying on the surface of the snow and angling towards the edge of the avalanche.

  2. Deploy Avalanche Safety Gear

    3. If you're caught in a larger avalanche, deploy your avalanche safety gear. Turn on your avalanche transceiver so rescuers can find you, and use your shovel and probe to dig yourself out of the snow.

  3. Try to Stay on the Surface

    4. If you're buried by the avalanche, try to stay on the surface of the snow by making swimming or rolling movements. This will create an air pocket around your face, giving you a chance to breathe while waiting for rescue.

  4. Stay Calm and Await Rescue

    5. Stay as calm as possible and wait for rescue. The more energy you exert, the faster you'll use up your oxygen supply. Use your whistle or shout to attract the attention of rescuers. In conclusion, avoiding avalanches and knowing what to do if you're caught in one is essential for safe skiing. Check avalanche forecasts, stay on designated ski runs, ski with a partner, carry avalanche safety gear, and know what to do in case of an avalanche. By following these guidelines, you can minimize the risks of skiing in avalanche-prone areas and enjoy a safe and enjoyable day on the slopes.

  5. Seek Medical Attention

    6. If you or someone in your group is caught in an avalanche, seek medical attention as soon as possible. Even if you feel fine, the shock and trauma of being caught in an avalanche can have long-lasting effects on your body.

  6. Learn First Aid Skills

    7. Knowing first aid skills can be a lifesaver in the event of an avalanche. Take a course in basic first aid, including CPR and rescue techniques, so you're prepared in case of an emergency.

  7. Respect the Environment

    8. Finally, it's important to respect the environment and the potential dangers that come with skiing in the mountains. Pay attention to warning signs, follow ski resort rules and regulations, and don't take unnecessary risks that could put yourself or others in danger.

    In conclusion, skiing in the mountains is an exhilarating experience, but it's important to take steps to avoid avalanches and know what to do if you're caught in one. Check avalanche forecasts, stay on designated ski runs, ski with a partner, carry avalanche safety gear, know what to do in case of an avalanche, seek medical attention, learn first aid skills, and respect the environment. By following these guidelines, you can stay safe and enjoy all the beauty and excitement that skiing in the mountains has to offer.

Which skis are best for the beginner skier - basic, semi-professional or professional?

April 2, 2023 by Scott

If you're new to skiing, you might be wondering what type of skis are best for beginners. While there are many different types of skis on the market, beginners should focus on selecting skis that are easy to maneuver and forgiving, rather than worrying about professional-grade equipment. In this blog, we'll take a closer look at the different types of skis and which ones are best for beginner skiers.

Basic Skis

Basic skis are typically shorter and more flexible than other types of skis, making them ideal for beginner skiers. They are designed to be forgiving and easy to maneuver, allowing you to focus on developing your skills and getting comfortable on the slopes. Basic skis are also more affordable than other types of skis, making them a great choice for those who are just starting out.

Semi-Professional Skis

Semi-professional skis are a step up from basic skis, offering more stability and control on the slopes. They are generally longer and stiffer than basic skis, which can make them more difficult to maneuver for beginners. However, semi-professional skis are also more responsive, allowing you to carve turns more easily and providing more stability at higher speeds.

Professional Skis

Professional skis are designed for experienced skiers who are looking for high performance on the slopes. They are typically longer and stiffer than other types of skis, with more advanced features like rockered tips and tails for better float in powder. Professional skis require a high level of skill and experience to use effectively, and are generally not recommended for beginner skiers.

Which Skis Are Best for Beginner Skiers?

For most beginner skiers, basic skis are the best choice. They are forgiving and easy to maneuver, which will help you develop your skills and gain confidence on the slopes. As you progress and become more comfortable skiing, you may want to consider upgrading to semi-professional skis, which will provide more stability and control at higher speeds.

When selecting skis, it's also important to consider your height, weight, and skiing ability. A reputable ski shop can help you select skis that are the right length and stiffness for your skill level and body type. Remember, the most important thing is to select skis that are comfortable and easy to use, so you can focus on enjoying your time on the slopes.

In conclusion, basic skis are the best choice for beginner skiers, offering forgiveness and ease of use that will help you develop your skills and gain confidence on the slopes. As you progress, you may want to consider upgrading to semi-professional skis, but it's important to select skis that are comfortable and easy to use, rather than worrying about professional-grade equipment. Happy skiing!
